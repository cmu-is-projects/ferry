About ferry

ferry is a command-line tool rubygem for data migrations and manipulations, maintained as an open-source project by the students of Carnegie Mellon's Information Systems department (currently Anthony Corletti and Logan Watanabe. The inspiration for ferry was kindled from collective internship experiences and from the growing prevalence of big data migration and manipulation challenges that companies, corporations, universities, and organizations face in today's information age.

It's important to note that not only are technology companies facing these big data issues, but that with the rise of the information age, more and more data is circulated around us, bringing these challenges to organizations that do not employ their own technical staff to manage these problems. The objective of ferry is to streamline the process of dealing with these big data problems, by making your data work for you, by entering a simple command from your terminal.

What can I use ferry for?

  • Data Migration & Manipulation
    • Exporting and importing data to and from various file formats (.csv, .yml, .sql, etc.)
    • Migrating data to and from various 3rd party storage services (Amazon S3, Google Cloud Storage, Rackspace, MS Azure, etc.)
    • Database switcher: Being able to quickly switch from one database type to another.
  • RESTful Database Command Line Tool
    • Alternative to Rails's ActiveRecord::Migration functionality

Please note that most of these features are still undergoing heavy development.

Our community goals entail;

  1. Having ferry included into a large production product, site, or system that is outside of ths IS department.
  2. Have ferry forked by multiple users once open-sourced.
  3. Continue to have the students of the Carnegie Mellon Information Systems department develop ferry in the future to provide lasting value and increase visibility of the CMU IS program in the professional and open-source community.
